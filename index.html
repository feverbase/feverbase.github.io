Feverbase

Finding the best way to beat COVID-19.

We're an international group of researchers collecting data about what's worked to fight COVID-19. We're particularly interested in how well repurposed drugs (e.g. chloroquine, hydroxychloroquine, favipiravir) work. We keep all data private and will collate the information into best practices for clinicians and policy-makers to treat the pandemic.

How can I help?

Send an email to private@feverbase.com with patient outcome information from your site. We're looking for patient information (such as age, sex, and comorbidities) and treatment information (drug, dose, timing) matched with the clinical outcome.

What will we do with the data?

We will create an anonymized dataset from your contributions, which we will analyze to make public recommendations about which drugs scientists should consider studying. If you would like to help analyze the data, please reach out. We will be releasing results after analysis and a quality review of the data.

Will you share the data or use it to publish papers?

No. We will not share your data and we will also not disclose your identity or affiliation, unless you expressly wish us to do that.

This project is a collaboration between researchers at Stanford, MIT, and around the world.
