Feverbase

Finding the best way to beat COVID-19.

We're an international group of researchers collecting data about what's worked to fight COVID-19. We're interested in the potential for repurposed drugs (e.g. chloroquine, hydroxychloroquine, favipiravir) to reduce or eliminate COVID-19 symptoms. Clinics across the world are beginning to use these drugs but sample sizes remain too small to draw conclusions about what works. By pooling together data from all sites at Feverbase, we can accumulate knowledge and provide well-sourced information reviewed by teams of scientists.

We need your help collecting and understanding data about how we fight COVID-19. We keep all data fully secure and private and will collate the information into best practices for clinicians and resource-allocators to fight this pandemic.

How can I help?

Send an email to private@feverbase.com with patient outcome information from your site. We're looking for patient information (such as age, sex, and comorbidities) and treatment information (drug, dose, timing) matched with the clinical outcome (e.g. quantitatively measured changes in symptom severity, duration, survival). We appreciate — and badly need — negative as well as positive results.

What will we do with the data?

We will create an anonymized dataset from your contributions, which we will analyze to make public recommendations about which drugs seem to work best in treating COVID-19. We want to answer two questions:

  1. What treatments should clinicians use to prevent or treat COVID-19?
  2. What drug manufacturing does society need to invest in to best beat COVID-19?

All parts of our analysis are going to be public, so everyone can benefit. If you would like to help analyze the data, please reach out. We will be releasing results periodically after analysis and a quality review of the data.

Will you share the data or use it to publish papers?

We will produce frequent blog posts about what seems to be working based on the data we collect. We will not be publishing it into academic journals, nor disclosing your identity or affiliation.
